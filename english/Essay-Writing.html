ESSAY WRITING USING ADJECTIVES

What is an Essay?

An essay is a short piece of writing on a particular topic. In your exam, you will write an essay using adjectives to make your writing more interesting and descriptive.

What are Adjectives?

Adjectives are words that describe or give more information about a noun (person, place, thing, or animal).

Remember: Adjectives make your writing colorful and vivid!

Types of Adjectives

  1. Size: big, small, tiny, huge, large, little
  2. Color: red, blue, beautiful, green, white, dark
  3. Shape: round, square, triangular, oval, circular
  4. Quality/Character: good, bad, honest, brave, kind, lazy
  5. Quantity: many, few, some, all, one, two
  6. Age: old, new, young, ancient, modern

How to Use Adjectives in Essays

  • Use adjectives after linking verbs: The sky is blue.
  • Use adjectives before nouns: I have a red ball.
  • Use adjectives with "very" or "really": She is very kind.
Example 1:
Without adjectives: "I went to a garden."
With adjectives: "I went to a beautiful garden full of colorful flowers."

Example 2:
Without adjectives: "The boy ran fast."
With adjectives: "The brave and energetic boy ran very fast."

Essay Format

  1. Introduction: Introduce the topic (2-3 sentences)
  2. Main Body: Give details and describe (4-5 sentences)
  3. Conclusion: End with a summary (2-3 sentences)

Sample Essay: My School

My school is a beautiful place of learning. It is a large building with spacious classrooms and green gardens. The teachers are kinder and helpful. I have many friends in my school. It is the best place for me to learn and grow.

Common Essay Topics for Exam

  • My School
  • My Best Friend
  • My Hobby
  • A Rainy Day
  • My Country
  • My Favorite Teacher
  • A Visit to a Zoo

5 Tips for Good Marks

  1. Use at least 5-6 adjectives in your essay
  2. Keep your essay in paragraphs
  3. Spell all words correctly
  4. Stay on the topic
  5. Check your grammar and punctuation
