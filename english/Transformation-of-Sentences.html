TRANSFORMATION OF SENTENCES

What is Sentence Transformation?

Changing a sentence from one form to another without changing its meaning is called Sentence Transformation.

Important: The meaning must remain the same after transformation.

Affirmative to Negative

AffirmativeNegative
All boys are present.No boy is absent.
Everyone loves him.Nobody hates him.
He is always late.He is never on time.
I like both books.I don't like either book.
He is wiser than his brother.His brother is not so wise as him.

Negative to Affirmative

NegativeAffirmative
She does not like either.She likes both.
He is not a fool.He is wise.
I have no money.I am penniless.
It is not possible.It is impossible.
He cannot deny it.He cannot but deny it.

Interrogative to Assertive

InterrogativeAssertive
Is she a teacher?She is a teacher.
Didn't he come?He came.
Who can help me?No one can help me.
Was the exam easy?The exam was easy.

Simple to Compound

SimpleCompound
Being tired, he stopped.He was tired, so he stopped.
In spite of being rich, he is sad.He is rich, but he is sad.
To win, you must try.You must try or you will win.

Degrees of Comparison

PositiveComparativeSuperlative
He is tall.He is taller than me.He is the tallest.
This is a good book.This is a better book.This is the best book.
She is beautiful.She is more beautiful.She is the most beautiful.
Ali runs fast.Ali runs faster.Ali runs fastest.

Changing Degrees

  • Positive to Comparative: "He is very wise." → "He is wiser than others."
  • Comparative to Superlative: "This is better than that." → "This is the best."
  • Superlative to Comparative: "He is the smartest." → "He is smarter than anyone else."

5 Tips for Exam

  1. Read the question carefully
  2. Identify what change is required
  3. Keep the meaning same
  4. Use correct grammar
  5. Check your answer twice
