ACTIVE AND PASSIVE VOICE

What is Voice?

Voice shows whether the subject does the action or receives the action.

  • Active Voice: Subject does the action
  • Passive Voice: Subject receives the action
Active: Ali writes a letter.
Passive: A letter is written by Ali.

How to Change Active to Passive

  1. Move the object to the beginning
  2. Add "is/am/are" before the verb
  3. Change verb to past participle (-ed form)
  4. Add "by" before the subject
TenseActivePassive
Simple PresentHe writes a book.A book is written by him.
Simple PastShe bought a car.A car was bought by her.
Present ContinuousThey are painting the house.The house is being painted by them.
Present PerfectWe have finished the work.The work has been finished by us.
Simple FutureI will open the door.The door will be opened by me.

Passive Voice Rules

  • Use "by" + object: Active: John helped me. → Passive: I was helped by John.
  • With "let": Let him go. → Let him be allowed to go.
  • With "make": Make him work. → He is made to work.
  • With "see/hear": I saw him run. → He was seen to run.

When to Use Passive Voice

  • When we don't know who did the action
  • When the action is more important than the person
  • In formal writing and scientific reports
  • In instructions and recipes
Remember: Preposition is always "by" not "from" in passive voice.

Exam Questions

Change the following into passive voice:

  1. The teacher teaches us English. → English is taught to us by the teacher.
  2. Someone broke the window last night. → The window was broken last night.
  3. Sara is writing a letter. → A letter is being written by Sara.

Change the following into active voice:

  1. The cake was eaten by the children. → The children ate the cake.
  2. A new school is being built in our town. → Someone is building a new school in our town.

Important Points to Remember

  1. Transitive verbs (with objects) can be changed to passive
  2. Intransitive verbs cannot be changed to passive
  3. Modal verbs: can + be + past participle
  4. Keep the same tense when changing voice
