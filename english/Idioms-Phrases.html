IDIOMS AND PHRASES

What are Idioms and Phrases?

An idiom is a group of words whose meaning is different from the individual words. A phrase is a group of words that work together.

Important: In exams, you are asked to give the meaning of given idioms/phrases.

Common Idioms and Meanings

At odds with = In disagreement with
At a loss = Not knowing what to do
At last = Finally
Back to square one = Start again
Bite the bullet = Face difficulty bravely
Break the ice = Make first contact
Burn the midnight oil = Work late at night
Call it a day = Stop working
Cut the coat according to cloth = Work within limits
Hit the nail on the head = Be exactly right
In the long run = Eventually
Kill two birds with one stone = Achieve two things at once
Lost heart = Become discouraging
Once in a blue moon = Very rarely
On the ball = Alert and capable
Pack of lies = Completely false
Piece of cake = Very easy task
Rain cats and dogs = Rain heavily
Take to one's heels = Run away
Under the weather = Feeling ill

Common Phrases and Meanings

  • As clear as day = Very clear
  • As alike as two peas = Exactly similar
  • As brave as a lion = Very brave
  • As cold as ice = Very cold
  • As white as snow = Very white
  • As busy as a bee = Very busy
  • As green as grass = Inexperienced
  • As hard as rock = Very hard

How to Answer Exam Questions

  1. Read the sentence carefully
  2. Find the idiom/phrase in the sentence
  3. Think of its meaning
  4. Choose the closest meaning from options
Tip: If you don't know the meaning, try to guess from context.

Use Idioms in Your Writing

You can use idioms in essay writing and letters to get better marks:

  • "It was a piece of cake for him."
  • "I lost heart when I saw the difficult exam."
  • "He burned the midnight oil to pass the exam."
  • "The task was as easy as pie."
