DO AS DIRECTED

What is "Do As Directed"?

In this question type, you are given a sentence and an instruction. You must change the sentence according to the given instruction.

Key: Read the instruction carefully and apply it correctly.

Common Types of Directions

DirectionExample
Change to Plural"The child is playing." → "The children are playing."
Change to Singular"The boys are running." → "The boy is running."
Change to Feminine"He is a boy." → "She is a girl."
Change to Masculine"She is a girl." → "He is a boy."
Change to Comparative"This is a big book." → "This is a bigger book."
Change to Superlative"This is a big book." → "This is the biggest book."
Change to Positive"She is wiser than him." → "She is not so wise as him."
Change to Comparative"He is very kind." → "He is kinder than most men."
Change to Interrogative"He is a student." → "Is he a student?"
Change to Exclamatory"What a beautiful scene!" → "How beautiful the scene is!"
Use "used to""He lives in Karachi." → "He used to live in Karachi."

Examples for Practice

  1. Use article "a" before word:
    "I have book." → "I have a book."
  2. Use "too" instead of "very":
    "He is very tall." → "He is too tall to wear."
  3. Begin with "If":
    "Study hard, you will pass." → "If you study hard, you will pass."
  4. Use "shall have to":
    "You must work hard." → "You shall have to work hard."

Sentence Types with Examples

1. Assertive to Interrogative:
"She is a doctor." → "Is she a doctor?"

2. Assertive to Exclamatory:
"It is a nice picture!" → "What a nice picture it is!"

3. Simple to Complex:
"I saw him. I called him." → "I saw him and called him."

4. Direct to Indirect:
"He said, 'I am happy.'" → "He said that he was happy."

5 Tips for Exam

  1. Read the instruction twice before answering
  2. Keep the meaning same while changing form
  3. Don't add or remove important words
  4. Check spelling and punctuation
  5. Make sure grammar is correct in the answer
